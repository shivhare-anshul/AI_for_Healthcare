Optical Coherence Tomography

An end to end platform to analyse and process OCT images. The Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) data are collected using CIRRUS. CIRRUS is a device that is used to take these scans. Each OCT cube is a collection of images (a.k.a. B-scan), 1 B-scan contain 128 images/frames. The retinal OCT data is used for the diagnosis of multiple retinal diseases, like Diabetic Macular Edema (DME), Age-related Macular Degeneration (AMD), Retinal Vein Occlusion (RVO). The OCT B-scan captures the retinal layers. Thus the structural changes of these layers are easily identifiable in the OCT B-scans. Such structural changes occur due to the deposition of the retinal fluids like Subretinal Fluid (SRF), Intraretinal Fluid (IRF), Pigmented Epithelial Detachment (PED). These fluids get deposited in the retina, and various retinal diseases occur.
